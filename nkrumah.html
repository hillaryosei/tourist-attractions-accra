The Park & Mausoleum is dedicated to the 1st President of Ghana Kwame Nkrumah and is the site where he officially declared Ghana’s independence. It was built in 1992. The mausoleum was designed by Don Arthur and harbors the bodies of both Kwame Nkrumah and his wife Fathia Nkrumah. The building is meant to represent an upside-down sword, the Akan people’s symbol of peace. The mausoleum has a black star at the top to represent unity. The mausoleum is surrounded by water, to symbolize life.
